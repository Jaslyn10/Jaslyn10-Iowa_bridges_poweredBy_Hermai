How this was built, and what “Poor” means

“Poor” is the government’s own term — it does not mean unsafe or about to collapse. Under the FHWA Pavement and Bridge Condition Performance Measures rule (2017), a bridge’s condition is set by the lowest of its deck, superstructure, substructure, or culvert ratings on the 0–9 National Bridge Inventory scale: 7–9 is Good, 5–6 is Fair, and 4 or below is Poor (a rating of 4 means “advanced section loss, deterioration, spalling or scour”). A Poor rating flags a bridge that needs major repair and gets extra inspection; as transportation agencies put it, poor condition does not mean a bridge is unsafe to use — unsafe bridges are weight-posted or closed. Definition: FHWA, Tables of Frequently Requested NBI Information (fhwa.dot.gov/bridge/britab.cfm) and the NBI Recording & Coding Guide.
